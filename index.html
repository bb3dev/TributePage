Jackie Robinson

MLB: Breaking the color barrier.

In 1947, Jackie Robinson engineered the integration of professional sports in America by breaking the color barrier in baseball.

Did you know?

  • Jackie Robinson broke the color barrier when he became the first black athlete to play Major League Baseball in the 20th century.
  • He signed with the Brooklyn Dodgers in 1947, and was named Rookie of the Year that year, National League MVP in 1949 and a World Series champ in 1955.
  • Before becoming a professional baseball player, Jackie Robinson played football for the Honolulu Bears.
  • Jackie Robinson had an older brother, Matthew, who won a silver medal in the 200-meter dash at the 1936 Olympics. He came in second to Jesse Owens.
  • After retiring from baseball, Robinson helped establish the African American-owned and -controlled Freedom Bank.
  • He retired in 1957 with a career batting average of .311. Robinson died in Connecticut in 1972.
